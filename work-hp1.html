Chapter 1: The Owl At Dawn

Harry had expected silence after the war, not a tap of talons at his bedroom window before sunrise. The snowy owl waiting outside carried a sealed Hogwarts letter stamped with the Headmistress crest and one line in Professor McGonagall's neat writing: "Mr. Potter, we require your immediate assistance."

By noon, Harry stood at the gates with Hermione and Ron, each of them pretending they were not relieved to be back. The castle looked the same, but the students whispering in the corridors carried a different fear. Wands were missing from locked trunks. Portraits had gone silent in the west tower.

McGonagall met them in her office, where three confiscated dark objects lay on her desk inside a ring of protective charms. "Someone is collecting artifacts tied to unfinished oaths," she said. "And Hogwarts is the next stop."

That evening, Harry took first watch in the Astronomy Tower. The wind was sharp enough to sting his eyes. Below, torchlight moved where no patrol should have been. He raised his wand, heart pounding, and followed the light down the spiral stairs.

Maya glanced out the window of the control room, where the sun was just beginning to peek over the horizon, painting the New Mexico desert in shades of gold and amber. "Beautiful as always. Though I imagine your view beats mine."

"I don't know about that," Commander Alex Chen replied, and Maya could hear the smile in his voice. "I'd trade all the stars in the galaxy for a sunrise on Earth right about now."

For a moment, neither of them spoke. The silence between worlds was never truly empty, only filled with things too vast to name. Static. Light-years. Unfinished thoughts.

Maya leaned closer to the console and checked the waveform data. "Your oxygen cycle looks stable. Power grid's good. We also received the imaging package you transmitted last night."

"And?" Alex asked.

"And I think you just sent me the most beautiful photograph anyone's ever taken of Saturn."

Alex laughed softly through the speaker. "Then make sure they put my name under it."

Chapter 2: The Vow Room

The hidden chamber was beneath the old dueling hall, sealed behind stonework no map recorded. Hermione translated the runes as Ron held his wand high for light: vows, promises, and magical debts, all carved into black marble that drank the glow from their spells.

"This is not a vault," Hermione whispered. "It's a ledger."

Harry stepped forward and his scar flared hot, not with pain this time but recognition.

At the center sat a silver basin, its surface rippling without water. Names formed and vanished on the metal. Some belonged to Death Eaters. Some belonged to people Harry trusted. When his own name appeared, a second line burned underneath: "Debt unresolved. Witness required."

Behind them, the chamber door slammed shut. Somewhere in the dark, a voice laughed softly and said, "Now we can begin."

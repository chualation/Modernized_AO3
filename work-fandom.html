Chapter 1: First Contact

The communication array crackled to life at 600 hours, precisely as it had every morning for the past three months. Maya adjusted her headset and took a sip of lukewarm coffee, watching the monitors flicker with telemetry data from the ISS-7 station orbiting Mars.

"Ground Control, this is ISS-7. Do you read me?" The voice was warm, familiar, tinged with the slight delay that came from transmitting across millions of miles of empty space.

"ISS-7, this is Ground Control. Reading you loud and clear, Commander Chen." Maya allowed herself a small smile as she spoke into her microphone. "How are things up there?"

"Oh, you know. Same stars, different day." There was a pause, filled with the static hum of space. "How's Earth treating you?"

Maya glanced out the window of the control room, where the sun was just beginning to peek over the horizon, painting the New Mexico desert in shades of gold and amber. "Beautiful as always. Though I imagine your view beats mine."

"I don't know about that," Commander Alex Chen replied, and Maya could hear the smile in his voice. "I'd trade all the stars in the galaxy for a sunrise on Earth right about now."

For a moment, neither of them spoke. The silence between worlds was never truly empty, only filled with things too vast to name. Static. Light-years. Unfinished thoughts.

Maya leaned closer to the console and checked the waveform data. "Your oxygen cycle looks stable. Power grid's good. We also received the imaging package you transmitted last night."

"And?" Alex asked.

"And I think you just sent me the most beautiful photograph anyone's ever taken of Saturn."

Alex laughed softly through the speaker. "Then make sure they put my name under it."

Chapter 2: Return Signal

By the time the next transmission window opened, Maya had already replayed their last conversation in her head a dozen times. She told herself it was because protocol mattered, because every line and every pause could contain something operational. It had nothing to do with the way his voice lingered after the channel closed.

"ISS-7, this is Ground Control. You're coming through," she said, steadier than she felt.

"Good," Alex replied. "I was hoping the universe would let me talk to you again."

Maya smiled before she could stop herself. On the monitor, rows of numbers scrolled downward like rain. Outside, morning light painted the desert gold. Somewhere far beyond that sky, a single voice crossed the silence to find her.

"Then maybe," she said, "we keep talking until it doesn't feel distant at all."

The transmission carried silence for a heartbeat, then a smile she could hear clearly even across millions of miles. "Copy that, Ground Control."

Chapter 1: The Missing Proof

Hermione Granger had read every Ministry paper on experimental warding in the last fifty years, and she hated each one. Not because they were wrong, exactly, but because they all circled the same gap: no one had ever proven how memory and magic drift apart after trauma.

In the oldest records vault, she found a sealed case marked with the Black family crest and a notation from the Unspeakables: "Archive unstable. Do not review without two witnesses." Hermione ignored the warning long enough to cast a diagnostic charm and watched the case answer back with runes she had never seen in any textbook.

"You're not opening that alone," came a familiar voice from the doorway. Draco Malfoy, now assigned to archival compliance, stepped inside with a stack of forms and a look that suggested he expected disaster. Hermione handed him a quill. "Good. Then sign as witness."

"Oh, you know. Same stars, different day." There was a pause, filled with the static hum of space. "How's Earth treating you?"

Maya glanced out the window of the control room, where the sun was just beginning to peek over the horizon, painting the New Mexico desert in shades of gold and amber. "Beautiful as always. Though I imagine your view beats mine."

"I don't know about that," Commander Alex Chen replied, and Maya could hear the smile in his voice. "I'd trade all the stars in the galaxy for a sunrise on Earth right about now."

For a moment, neither of them spoke. The silence between worlds was never truly empty, only filled with things too vast to name. Static. Light-years. Unfinished thoughts.

Maya leaned closer to the console and checked the waveform data. "Your oxygen cycle looks stable. Power grid's good. We also received the imaging package you transmitted last night."

"And?" Alex asked.

"And I think you just sent me the most beautiful photograph anyone's ever taken of Saturn."

Alex laughed softly through the speaker. "Then make sure they put my name under it."

Chapter 2: The Equation Of Oaths

The case opened with a hiss of displaced air and revealed a single parchment covered edge to edge in moving numbers. Arithmancy symbols rearranged themselves each time Hermione blinked, as if the proof refused to stay solved for long.

"It's not just math," Hermione said. "It's binding language."

Draco leaned in, tracing one glyph with his wand. "Then whose oath is this?"

The parchment flashed and projected a spectral timeline across the room. Events from the war appeared, then shifted toward dates in the future neither of them recognized. At the center of the timeline sat one immutable line: "Counter-vow due at midnight, St. Mungo's bell tower."

Hermione rolled the parchment and stood. "Then we have five hours to rewrite a magical contract that should have ended years ago."
